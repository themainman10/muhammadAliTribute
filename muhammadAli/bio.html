The boxing career of Muhummad Ali

Muhammad Ali was born january 17 1942 in Louisville Kentucky as Cassius Clay, however he later changed his name to Muhammad Ali. Ali became involved in boxing at a young age and went to the olympic and won the gold medal for boxing at age 18. Later at age 22 Ali won the world heavyweight championship.

1971 Ali lost for the first time in his career to joe frazier. This was a major fight in boxing history and most people would have just given up after a loss like that. However Ali came back and fought against Fazers two more times and he won both times. Ali went on to retire in 1976 and he was diagnosed with Parkinson’s disease in 1984. Ali passed away due to respiratory issues in 2016.
